The card game

Pokemon Cards

  • Purpose: Represent Pokemon that players use to battle.
  • Types:
    •
  • Basic Pokemon: Can be played directly onto the Bench.
  • Evolution Pokemon: Must be played on top of a corresponding Basic Pokemon or Stage 1 Pokemon to evolve it (e.g., Stage 1 and Stage 2 Pokemon).
  • Special Pokemon: Includes Pokemon with unique attributes, such as:
  • EX/GX/V/VMAX: High-power Pokemon cards with extra abilities.
  • Radiant, Shiny, or Full Art: Unique designs or rare variants with high collector value.
  • Stats and Features:
  • HP (Hit Points): The Pokemon's health.
  • Attacks: Moves that require Energy cards to activate.
  • Weakness & Resistance: Specific types of Pokemon they are strong or weak against.

Trainer Cards

  • Purpose: Provide tools, strategies, and effects to assist in gameplay.
  • Types:
    •
  • Supporter Cards: Allow powerful effects but are limited to one per turn (e.g., drawing cards, searching for Pokemon).
  • Item Cards: Provide various utilities like healing or searching cards without restrictions on usage per turn.
  • Stadium Cards: Affect both players, changing the battle environment.
  • Tool Cards: Attach to Pokemon to enhance their abilities or grant new effects.
  • Usage: Integral to strategy; they often help players draw cards, retrieve Pokemon, or disrupt the opponent.

    Energy Cards

  • Purpose: Power up a Pokemon's attacks.
  • Types:
    •
  • Basic Energy: Includes Grass, Fire, Water, Lightning, Psychic, Fighting, Darkness, Metal, and Fairy. These are the core energy types.
  • Special Energy: Offer unique effects in addition to providing energy. Examples include:
  • Double Colorless Energy: Provides two Colorless Energy.
  • Aurora Energy: Can act as any type of Energy but with an additional cost, such as discarding a card.
  • Essential for Attacks: Most Pokemon attacks have specific Energy requirements.

    Special Cards

  • Purpose: Unique cards that offer advanced gameplay options or high collectible value.
  • Types:
    •
  • Promo Cards: Exclusive cards distributed at events, in special collections, or promotional campaigns.
  • Rainbow Rares: Cards with a rainbow-colored holographic design.
  • Full Art Cards: Feature expanded artwork over the card's surface.
  • Alternate Art Cards: Feature alternative artwork, often highly collectible.
  • Secret Rares: Cards with a collector number higher than the set total (e.g., 121/120), making them rare and sought after.
  • Gold Cards: Ultra-rare cards with a golden design.
  • Character Cards: Combine Pokemon with their Trainers in a unique artistic design.
