Pokemon Crystal

  • Cover-Pokemon: Suicune
  • Release: 2. November 2001
  • Region: Johto
  • Plattform: Game Boy Color
  • New Pokémon: 100

Villain Team

Team Rocket is a group of villains in the Pokémon games Red, Blue, and Yellow. They are an evil organization that wants to use Pokémon for their own profit and power. Led by their boss, Giovanni, they steal Pokémon, run secret operations, and cause trouble wherever they go. You meet their members, including the iconic duo Jessie and James in Yellow, throughout the story. Your goal is to stop their plans and show them that using Pokémon for good is always better!

Story and Features

Pokémon Crystal follows the same main story as Gold & Silver: the player, a young trainer from New Bark Town, travels through Johto, battles eight Gym Leaders, stops Team Rocket, and challenges the Elite Four. However, Crystal introduces a special Suicune storyline. Instead of roaming, Suicune appears in key locations before a final battle at Tin Tower. A new character, Eusine, follows Suicune, trying to understand its power. Both Lugia and Ho-Oh are available but at different levels (Lugia at level 60, Ho-Oh at level 40). The game also introduces the Battle Tower, a female playable character (Kris), improved Pokémon animations, and small cutscenes. After defeating Champion Lance, the player can travel to Kanto, battle its Gym Leaders, and challenge Red at Mt. Silver. In Pokémon Crystal, players can choose to play as a girl for the first time. In Gold & Silver, you could only play as a boy, but now you can select Kris, the first female trainer in Pokémon history. This change made the game more inclusive. Pokémon Crystal introduces 100 new Pokémon, which brings the total number of Pokémon to 251. It also adds two new types, Dark and Steel, to make battles more interesting. The game features a day and night cycle, so some Pokémon only appear in the morning, while others show up at night. Breeding is another new feature: if you leave two compatible Pokémon at the Day Care, they can produce an Egg, which hatches into a baby Pokémon. Finally, after you win the Johto League, you can travel to the Kanto region and challenge the original Gym Leaders from Pokémon Red and Blue. Another new feature is the Battle Tower, a special place where players can battle strong trainers with different rules. Trainers must use Pokémon at certain levels and win multiple battles in a row. This was not in Gold & Silver and gave players a new challenge after finishing the main game.
